When I was in college, there was a socio-political movement represented by the mantra “Think Globally, Act Locally.” It applied to any social, political, or environmental efforts and stressed the importance of acting first in your own backyard. I had that bumper sticker on my car, and as I grew into the world, I always approached any work I was doing to move society forward with a local focus first.

I still apply that philosophy to my work in the local queer community. Although we have so much work left to do we also have every reason to be proud of all the work we’ve already done.

I look around at our Seven Cities, and each one has some sort of visible Pride organization or event. I also see active Pride organizations in smaller cities and towns around the Commonwealth producing their own highly visible events

I look up to Richmond and see a new blue administration which has our best interests at heart, and I see Equality Virginia continuing to do the heavy lifting on the legislative level.

All that having been said we’re not out of the woods yet. Just last month the Chesapeake School Board passed a policy that bars employees from asking about or using students’ preferred pronouns or chosen names unless parents explicitly request it.

Also last month in Virginia Beach, vandals defaced the rainbow crosswalk in the ViBe district.

So yes, let’s continue to support those local and state decision-makers and politicians who support us. Let’s continue to donate to those global efforts to make the world better. And ALWAYS vote.

But let’s also continue the work in our neighborhoods and our towns that make Hampton Roads one of the most diverse metropolitan communities in the country.
